Places to Visit in Chicago

Sometime around February most Chicagoans start questioning why they put up with the cold. Summertime, however, quickly reminds them why this is one of the greatest cities on Earth. Chicago has two speeds, winter and summer. In summer, it is hard to find a free weekend from all the festivals, concerts, and activities. There is a gorgeous beach less than a 20 minute walk from downtown. Winter is the necessary downtime allowing you to catch up on sleep and save a little money before the weather picks up and it starts all over again. Below are some easy sights to see while in town and a selection of our favorite restaurants.




View Sights to See for Out of Towners in a larger map

The Bean

What the heck is a bean? This shiny, slightly amorphous, sculpture officially known as the Cloud Gate is located in Millenium Park nestled between downtown and the lake in the Loop. Although answers as to why this piece of artwork is so endearing range from it's cute shape, to the reflective pictures which are a must, I belive it has to do with what I have termed "the Vortex." If you have time to see this iconic piece of Chicago, I strongly encourage you to fight the crowd to walk underneath it. When you look straight up, while directly underneath the sculpture, I promise you will understand why I coined the term.



The Park

While you are at the bean, check out the rest of Millenium Park. From the beautiful Lurie Park outside of the Art Institute of Chicago's modern wing, to the Crown Fountain featuring large fountains that diplay Faces of Chicago, there are plenty of sights within a 20-30 minute walking distance.
